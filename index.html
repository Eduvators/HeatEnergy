Heat Energy Transformation

In electrical circuits, loads consume electrical energy and convert it into various forms of energy. One important transformation is the conversion to heat energy.

Heat Energy

Electrical energy can be converted into thermal energy when it encounters resistance in a circuit.

Example: When electricity passes through a heating element, it encounters resistance, causing the element to heat up and generate thermal energy. This is how PTC (Positive Temperature Coefficient) heaters work to warm up vehicle batteries, powertrains, and passenger cabins.
